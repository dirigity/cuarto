Why is Africa so Poor
 Everyone knows that africa is poor, but most people don’t understand why. When you think about africa you do not imagine high flying jobs and people dressed up elegantly, you imagine hunger, wars, epidemics. In reality there are really rich people in africa, but their are a really small minority. They are the owners of the crop fields that feed the world, but the normal people, the ones that are affected the most, are forced to work in those crop fields under miserable conditions and really low salaries. This may have been the heavier factor of them all, but it is not the only one.
In the areas where people live far away from big businesses like plantations, they earn for a living by being farmers in farms that produce just enough for them and their families. In theory this should be a normal life, but the problem is that people fight each other to steal their animals. This happens because population grows and animals do not keep up. When people try to defend their plantations and properties they often get hurted and unabled to do their jobs. This produces that a big part of the population can’t help anymore to the social economy and drags it to poverty.
Furthermore, europe is not helping at all. Africa is the richest country in the world when it comes to raw materials such as gold or coltan, but the other countries buy it under its real price so they can get even richer.  Most people are not aware of all the difficulties that refugees have to go through to reach countries with Spain. So we contacted a Syrian refugee who is residing in Valencia and is part of an awareness campaign to accept more refugees.

When did you decided to leave your country?
It was a very delicate situation. It wasn’t in my home waiting to a bomb exploding, I was in the front line. I stopped doing the military service and abandoned Syria because I either killed or killed me. It is a civil war, Syrians against Syrians. It was not my place.
Was it from one day to the next?
The tension was lived for many years, already with the coup d'etat of Al Asad. In 2010 the checkpoints started, too much police in the street ... And I exploded.We were thrown at a deep end and I was the only one that arrived alive. In 2011 I came. I had not thought of it, just either escape or finish with a bullet in my temple
Where is residing your family?
My mother and maternal family had been living outside for years. My uncle came to work and study, he left the country during the armed conflict of 1983. My grandparents came to visit him, but because of my grandfather's illness they stayed here. My father is still in Syria because he and his family is involved in politics. There I also have an 11 year old sister and a stepbrother.
How did your father's family feel about your escape?
Pretty bad. For them, I deserted, and they had problems with the secret police and the military police. We have also clashed a lot about political issues: we think differently. They are with the government and I am not against only the regime, but against everything that is happening. Right now you can not be neutral, or you are from one side or from another. And everyone has their hands stained.

As we, see refugees like Sami Shihadeh are trying to escape from this horrible situation and we are not giving them the opportunity of living as normal citizens. But Syrian refugees like him are even more prepared than us for doing for example politics, because they already know which things will lead to a bad political situation. EU Actuation
 in 2015 a contract was signed between turkey and the EU in order to fix the situation of the syrian refugees. Right now Turkey is the main entrance path way for refugees to get to Europe. This means that Turkey has a lot in the plate, because the people that get to their frontiers are homeless and in most cases they have no money at all.
Turkey government needs help to administer this difficult situation. In this contract EU offers economical help to end this problem with the only condition of it being spend on the syrian crisis. Everyone wishes that this cooperation lasts a long time, because it may be the solution to the biggest problem that the world is facing right now. You can find the complete document here
This is not the first document of its kind. There have been other documents where a group of different countries accorded how many people were they going to let in, but most of them have disappeared in a big and useless bureaucracy. Hopefully this document is not one of a kind and gets repeated more in the future.
The last document of this kind was signed in 2015, and since then there have been no more guvernamental projects to try to fix this situation. This is the reason why we encourage people to get the ball rolling and sign this petition in change.org. The petition asks the EU to react to the situation this people are living

We are students from a high school of spain, and we are really worried about the situation that refugees are suffering nowadays. The bottom line of this project is to aware the most people possible about the challenging conditions that some people are being forced to go through to ensure a better future. People think that refugees come only because of wars but there are other causes that motivate people to risk their lives to emigrate like for example african people want to escape from the epidemics that destroy their countries. But when refugees get to europe they hit a death end because our society isn’t ready to admit that much people. The only solution that could be accomplished in a near future is to make the country they belong a safer place. This is the purpose of our campaign is all about. We want to show people that the right way of doing things is to help before they need it, so they don’t have to get to a situation of need.
